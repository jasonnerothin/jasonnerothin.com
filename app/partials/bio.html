Jason was born in the mid-1970s, in rural mid-Minnesota, the first-born child of a physician and nurse. A sister and brother came 2 and 5 years later on.

Jason matriculated to St.Olaf College where he pursued a number of interesting studies - music, German, biology. He spent the summer between junior and senior years at the University of Minnesota in the crystallography lab of Dr. Leonard Banaszak, where he fell in love with the natural sciences. Eventually, he graduated with a degree in chemistry.

Jason set off for Ann Arbor, Michigan, in hopes of doing Great Scientific Things. During a brief and undistinguished career as a bench scientist — he has what they call “bad hands” — he worked his way through another two degrees at Eastern Michigan University, including, crucially, one in Computer Science. Since those days, Jason has happily been paid to do all sorts of fun and interesting things with computers, mostly of the “coding” variety.

While in Michigan, Jason met Shelley, the woman who would become his wife and the mother of their children. Three children and one cat have been added to the mix.

Jason has far more interests than time. Since graduating college, he's picked up a new hobby about once every other year: Cooking, Ruffed Grouse Hunting, Philip Ward's Lifetime Of Reading, fixin’ old trucks, gardening, money management, and singing in the Church choir are all going concerns.
